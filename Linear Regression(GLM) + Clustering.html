In order to determine the clusters of the passengers who have the similar characteristics we applied hierarchical clustering first.Since not all the attributes are not in the same unit we need to scale our data before applying clustering. To do this, we used scale function.And we used scaled data for clustering. We applied a complete linkage in this hierarchical clustering with Euclidean distance.To determine the number of clusters which are appropriate, we used the cutree function and gave 8 numbers to k from 1 to 8. We created 8 different scenarios. And then compare them by investigating the number of members of each cluster in each scenario. According to this scenario, the best number of clusters is 6. However, when we check the silhoutte index, best number of clusters is 2. If we want to have more than 2 clusters, then we can divide 6 clusters. In this question, we think that we have minimum 3-4 clusters to identify each groups and differentiate them from others.

We prepared special marketing plans and offers for each cluster. You can find them below:

For Least Frequent Flyers; They don’t fly as much as other clusters with East-West Airlines. We believe that the reason is mostly financial. Therefore, the airline should deliver them some special offers and cheap tickets. For instance, there may be 40% discount for ticket prices at special dates such as national holidays.

For Rewards Credit Card Users; We believe that they use rewards credit card a lot due to its mileage earning system. However, their balance is low, because they don’t use other ways to earn miles. They don’t fly frequently as well. Since, they like to earn miles, the airline may offer them other mileage earning channels such as frequent flyer credit cards or small business credit cards.

For Ordinary Users; This cluster may be in the scope of every marketing campaign, because they don’t have any prominent features. However, we suggest using more general campaigns rather than more specialized ones. For example, regular follow-up emails or promotions can be sent to this customer segment.

For Rich in Mile Users; They have lots of miles that can be used while purchasing flight tickets. Therefore, the airline may offer them to get reward tickets for a smaller number of miles. This campaign may increase their loyalty to the brand.

For Small Business Credit Card Users; Since, they are a unique and niche customer segment, the airline may address their “Small Business” title and by using this title, some customized marketing campaigns could be prepared. For example, some discount may be applied while purchasing flight tickets using small business credit cards only. Their loyalty is expected to rise by that way.

For Last 12 months’ Most Active Flyers; The customers in this segment are the ones who flied most in last 12 months. Therefore, their average annual income is expected to be higher compared to other segments. For this customer segment, the airline may deliver premium offers such as discount for business class seats or in-flight food purchases. Thus, East-West could increase its revenue.
